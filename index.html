Introduction

Arunachal Pradesh is a state in northeastern India. It was formed from the former region of the North-East Frontier Agency (NEFA) and became a state on February 20, 1987. It shares international borders with Bhutan to the west,

Myanmar to the east, and a disputed border with China to the north on the McMahon Line. Itanagar is the state capital of Arunachal Pradesh. This state is the largest of the seven sister states in northeastern India by region. It is the largest of the Seven Sister States of Northeast India by area.

3 Gender Sensitivity
Women in Arunachal have been playing a major role in agriculture, traditions, mythology etc. The concept of dowry doesn't exist. Instead, the groom's family gifts the bride's family with goods to compensate for taking the bride since daughters are considered an economic asset.

4 Water Conservation
The Department of Water Resources of Arunachal Pradesh conducted demonstration projects on rooftop rainwater harvesting and artificial groundwater recharge during the 11th Five-Year Plan, covering 235 projects in 11 districts.
The Apatani's System is a wet rice cultivation and fish farming system practiced in elevated regions of about 1600 m and gently sloping valleys. This system harvests both ground and surface water for irrigation. It is practiced by Apatani tribes of Ziro in the Lower Subansiri district of Arunachal Pradesh.
5 Resource Conservation
    Resource Conservation Methods
  • Richness of forest is maintained through traditional ecological methods and knowledge
  • Arunachal Pradesh has a large network of protected areas covering an area of 9,527.995 sq.km.
  • The Govt. of Arunachal Pradesh has constituted 9 Wildlife Sanctuaries, 2 National Parks and one Biosphere Reserve.
    Conserving Protected Areas
  • Most of the protected areas are away from any villages.
  • Almost all the protected areas are in rugged terrain and inaccessible geography.
  • There is an established relation between the Park / Sanctuaries authority and village Forest Development Council.
6 Ethics and Values
    Ethics and Values
  • There is a diverse range of religions in Arunachal including Christanity, Tibetan Buddhism, Theravada Buddhism, Hinduism etc.
  • Most of the tribes have been protecting and conserving sacred groves based on their religious culture, beliefs, totem, taboos and festivals.
  • The people of Arunachal Pradesh welcomed a newborn child into the world by placing certain symbols at the entrance door of their houses.
    Different Tribes and their Values
  • Monpas and Sherdukpens are known as the first group of people in Arunachal Pradesh. Head to these regions and know about their Lamaistic tradition of Mahayana Buddhism.
  • Followed by Monpas, the second tribal community comprises of Adis, Akas and Apatanis.They are ardent devotees of the Sun and the Moon God.
  • The Octes and Wanchos tribes mainly belong to the Tirap district. You can find their culture similar to the Vaishnavism of Arunachal Pradesh.
